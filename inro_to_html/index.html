Eastern Orthodoxy

The Eastern Orthodox Church is the ancient Christian church that is firmly rooted in the apostolic tradition. It is made up of a number of autocephalous churches (this meansthey are not subject to an external authority like a bishop from another church). The different churches are divided along national lines: Greek, Russian, Antiochian, etc. There is even a fledgling American church called The OCA, the Orthodox Church in America.

The Divine Liturgy

The Divine Liturgy is the cornestone of Orthodox worship. It commemorates the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. It is the service that Protestants would call communion and Catholics would call the Eucharist. The liturgy that is currently used was written by Saint John Chrysostom in the 5th century.
